10. 3D Machining

10.1. Available machining cycles

Arbitrary Stock Roughing: Z constant stock removal for stock models of any shape with the option of stock model update. Machining proceeds parallel to the specified contour or parallel to axis.

Optimized Roughing: Roughing and rest roughing of any workpieces. Calculate toolpaths for standard pocket shapes such as rectangular and circular pockets. The model geometry and stock model geometry will be taken into consideration to calculate highly efficient toolpaths and reduce direction changes ("high speed cutting"). Machine remaining rest material areas based on the generated "resulting stock".

Profile Finishing: Allows multi-surface, collision-free milling with different guide curve strategies; optional slope-dependent machining. XY optimized machining is offered for the X axis and Y axis machining profiles.

Z Level Finishing: Z constant finishing with optional slope-dependent machining. By adapting the vertical stepdown values to the surface flow especially for steep surfaces, unnecessary fine infeed increments are avoided and an optimal line distance is thus guaranteed.

Z Level Shape Finishing: Z-Level machining for steep areas. Optionally, plane machining can be replaced by cuts parallel to any shape.

Iso Machining: The milling paths follow the ISO lines (U, V) so that the toolpaths are optimally adapted to the surface curve.

Free Path Milling: Milling of freely defined 3D contours. Multiple vertical stepdown and ramped transition between milling paths are possible.

Plane Machining: Face Milling of planar surfaces with pocket strategy. Automatic plane level detection or manual selection of surfaces. The stockmodel can be used for trimming.

Complete Finishing: Z constant finishing with automatic pocket-shaped machining of flat areas.

Equidistant Finishing: Finishing with constant infeed on surface; particularly suitable for high-speed milling. Machining takes place equidistantly within a closed guide curve or flowing between two guide curves.

Form Pocket: Finishing of pockets with free-form floor.

Automatic Rest Machining: Targeted rework machining of individual rest material areas left during a finishing cycle.

Corner Rest Machining: Optimized removal of rest material in vertical corners and adjacent bottom surfaces within pocket and pocket-like geometries with constant corner radii.

Cutting Edge: Optimized machining of cutting edges using curve-based pre-roughing (strategy: manual curve) in 3D mode, followed by rest machining in 2D mode (strategy: reference cycle).

Pencil Milling: Automatic detection and machining of grooves.

Rework Machining: Machining of pre-calculated toolpaths from a reference job where the toolpaths could not be machined due to detected collisions. For the rework machining, a different tool is applied to avoid the previously detected collision areas from the reference job.

Rib / Groove Machining: Roughing and finishing of ribs and grooves or limited groups thereof in one job for side surfaces and floor areas respectively.

Parameters

Machining mode

Base the selection of the machining direction on the main extension of the surfaces.

X parallel (1): Machining in X direction, infeed in Y direction.

Y parallel (2): Machining in Y direction, infeed in (-X) direction.

Machining angle: inclined machining is possible by rotating the coordinate system around the Z axis. Specify the desired rotation angle.

Infeed mode

Zigzag direct: The machining direction changes per section. The infeed movement follows the shortest path. The horizontal stepover between two adjacent sections takes place with the machining feedrate (G1) (1).

Zigzag smooth: The machining direction changes per section. The infeed path resembles that of a loop (G2/G3 movement). This mode is primarily intended for HSC machining (2).

Oneway: the machining direction stays the same for each section (3). Machining can optionally take place in Conventional milling.

Infeed

Vertical stepdown (1): Z-Stepdown to the next machining plane, from the Z direction of the active frame.

Horizontal stepover: Infeed in the XY plane.

Stepover (factor of diameter) (2): Stepover in the XY plane as a factor of the cutter diameter (3).

Allowance

Allowance Z (4): Allowance in Z direction.

Macros

Return macro: Extend the approach and retract path of the tool by a distance (3) specified by the approach factor and retract factor in the plane of the current machining. Displayed in (1) for zigzag direct stepover mode, and in (2) for zigzag smooth. A factor > 0.5 for approach factor and retract factor ensures that before and after performing a cut, a tool is outside the area to be machined.

Overlap parameter

Consider corner radius: An additional cut is performed at the end of machining to prevent material remaining when using bullnose end mills.

Fit step: To improve the cut division for the tool, the steps are adjusted. Avoid rest material at the end of machining when using bullnose end mills.

(1) Consider corner radius and fit step not activated,

(2) Consider corner radius activated,

(3) Fit step activated,

(A) Stepover direction

Retract mode

Clearance plane (1): all vertical retract and stepdown movements to the next machining plane take place via the clearance plane.

Clearance distance / Lead-in position (2)

The stepdown movement to the next workplane is realized with Contour Milling at the clearance distance (A), with Pocket Milling at the lead-in position (start position + lead-in macro) without retraction in the Z direction (B). Approach and retract macros are executed at each machining plane.

Clearance

Select the clearance plane (1) as plane for rapid travel movements along all three axes (absolute dimensions). Below this plane, only the infeed in Z direction is in fast travel mode up to the clearance distance. For a 3D model, the clearance plane value can be taken from the drawing.

Warning

The clearance plane is always situated above the workpiece boundaries in the Z direction.

Define a clearance distance (2) as a distance from the current surface (3), for fast travel Z-stepdown infeed (relative dimensions). Below the clearance distance, Z-stepdown takes place at feedrate.

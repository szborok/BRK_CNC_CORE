5 axes

Inclination strategy

4X Machining: Depending on the blade geometry, it may be possible to calculate a fixed tilt for the second rotary axis. Access is then solely achieved by rotating about the Z axis.

Avoidance strategy

Around Z: This option activates the previously used behavior, where the collision avoidance is mainly implemented by pivoting around the Z-axis.

Advantage: Very simple movement of the tool that can lead to constant speed of the rotary axes.

Camberline: This option activates a new strategy for collision avoidance that is mainly implemented perpendicular to the camberline (centerline) of the blade.

Special options

Close to blade: Facilitates precise tool control near to the blade with optimized inclination and the lowest possible number of axis movements on the machine.

Precise avoidance: If this option is enabled, the mesh for collision avoidance will be calculated with approx. 20% greater accuracy than for point calculation of the toolpath, so that even a tool positioned vertically at the blade can typically move without collision between two long and closely placed blades.

Edge behavior

Min. angle - leading edge / Min. angle - trailing edge: Minimum angle that must be maintained on the leading or trailing edges. This helps to optimize the machining of different edge shapes.

Intermediate angle down / Intermediate angle up

Angle to Z: Defined angle of the tool to the Z axis for downwards and/or upwards movement at the critical location, usually between the main blade and the leading edge on the splitter

Iso position (from leading) / Iso position (from trailing): Specify the location where the angle defined relative to the Z axis is to be placed. The advantage of this method is that you may be able to use tools that are better suited for working in the small spaces between the blades. This allows you to increase the process parameters and thus reduce the machining time.

Frame angle limitation

Max. angle to Z: The frame angle limitation prevents the system from calculating a tool orientation that the machine cannot carry out.

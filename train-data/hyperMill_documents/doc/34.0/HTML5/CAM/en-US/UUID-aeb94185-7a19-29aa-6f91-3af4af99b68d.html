3.7. Structure CAM project

Structure a CAM project in the hyperMILL browser.

CAM Plan

The CAM Plan contains functions that simplify the programming process. These range from analyzing the part to displaying and repairing possible surface defects to the automatic generation of features and geometries required for programming. In addition, the CAM plan provides a platform for advanced technologies and more interactivity in programming. The CAM Plan is a dialog page in the hyperMILL browser, divided into a tree structure (in the upper part) and the Selections and Tasks areas (in the lower part).

Job list

Uppermost administration level in the hyperMILL job browser. If projects have a simple structure, the job list (work schedule) is usually sufficient for including all the jobs required to machine a workpiece in the correct sequence. Several job lists can be assigned to one CAD model. A job list can be transferred to other CAD models that have a similar machining sequence.

Compound job

Organize your job lists with compound jobs and clearly structure extensive CAM projects. Use compound jobs to structure transformations or assign individual transformations to several jobs. In contrast to job lists, which only permit mirrors, compound jobs also allow linear patterns and circular patterns in addition to mirrors.

Job

Lowermost administration level in the hyperMILL job browser. A hyperMILL job is a typical machining step within a joblist. The following machining strategies are available for defining jobs: Probing, Turning, Drilling, 2D Milling, 3D Milling, 5-axis Milling.

Linking job

Link jobs with each other in such a way that approach and retract movements for the entire machining sequence and all linking movements between the individual jobs can be optimized and monitored for collisions.

NC event

Structure your programming, independent of joblists and jobs, with control-specific functions and commands that are forwarded directly to the postprocessor.

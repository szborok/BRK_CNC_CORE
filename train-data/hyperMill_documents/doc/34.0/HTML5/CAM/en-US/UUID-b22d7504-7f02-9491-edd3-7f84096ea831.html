Strategy

Strategy

Slope angle: The delimitation of flat and steep areas is determined by defining a slope angle. If the option is not activated, the toolpaths are not divided into different tilt ranges.

Machining mode

All areas: Steep as well as flat areas are machined. Steep areas are machined either parallel to the contour using the option Parallel (1) or in Z levels with the option Z level (2). Flat areas are always machined parallel to the contour.

Steep areas: Only steep areas are machined, either parallel to the contour using the option Parallel (1) or in Z levels with the option Z level (2).

Flat areas (3): Only flat areas are machined parallel to the contour.

Corners and intersection areas

Automatic optimization is performed in corners and intersection areas of different toolpath segments. This ensures safe and clean machining of the existing rest material.

Cutting mode

Climb milling, Conventional milling: In the case of contour-parallel machining with continuous orientation, the following definitions apply: climb milling and conventional milling relate to clockwise rotating tools. Toolpath fillets

When using the Z level option, the toolpaths in the plane can be rounded by defining a value.

Blending

Smooth overlap: Smooth overlapping of the toolpaths between different machining areas ensures a very high surface quality in the area of transition from the surfaces of the vertical corners to the laterally adjacent surfaces. If the option is enabled, additional toolpaths (B) are added to the standard toolpath (A) in the transition areas.

(A) = Standard toolpath, (B) = Additional toolpath.

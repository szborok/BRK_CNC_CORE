Parameters

Infeed

Vertical stepdown (1): choose the stepdown to the next machining plane, from the Z direction of the active frame. This gives the number of machining planes. If a top is situated between two defined machining planes, the system will automatically reduce the vertical stepdown and insert an intermediate step.

Horizontal stepover factor (2): stepover in the XY plane is the distance between the center points of two adjacent milling tool paths and is generally given as a factor of the milling tool's diameter

Allowance

Choose Allowance XY (1) and Allowance Z (2). Remaining material that is to be removed in subsequent fine machining. Corresponds to the distance from the final contour that the cutter has to keep

Inclination

Wall angle (1): Pocket wall incline angle. If the job is linked with a pocket feature (with defined wall angle and active status), the preset value for the wall angle is adopted.

For further information on the Pocket feature, see section Pocket.

Retract mode

Clearance plane (1): all vertical retract and stepdown movements to the next machining plane take place via the clearance plane.

Clearance distance / Lead-in position (2)

The stepdown movement to the next workplane is realized with Contour Milling at the clearance distance (A), with Pocket Milling at the lead-in position (start position + lead-in macro) without retraction in the Z direction (B). Approach and retract macros are executed at each machining plane.

Clearance

Select the clearance plane (1) as plane for rapid travel movements along all three axes (absolute dimensions). Below this plane, only the infeed in Z direction is in fast travel mode up to the clearance distance. For a 3D model, the clearance plane value can be taken from the drawing.

Warning

The clearance plane is always situated above the workpiece boundaries in the Z direction.

Define a clearance distance (2) as a distance from the current surface (3), for fast travel Z-stepdown infeed (relative dimensions). Below the clearance distance, Z-stepdown takes place at feedrate.

Toolpath fillets

The toolpaths in the plane can be smoothed.

Fillet radius: Abrupt changes in direction within the cutting toolpaths are rounded with the specified radius (2).

Radius for horizontal infeed: The horizontal infeed between the toolpaths is rounded with the horizontal infeed radius (1).

Fillet all toolpaths: model contour corners are rounded with the radius defined above, with the result that the machining contour deviates from the model contour at the corners (3).

The CAD model

The basis for the creation of an NC program for 3D machining is the CAD model created in the CAD program and consisting of surfaces and/or solids. It contains all areas to be machined, as well as adjacent areas that are not to be machined in order to optimize the machining procedure and the collision check. 2D or 3D lines that are used as limitation contours or leading curves for profiles during the job definition.

Surface gaps

Make sure that no gaps arise between adjacent surfaces or between adjacent surfaces and solids during model creation. Large surface gaps can cause unwanted nose-diving of the cutter in the course of machining. Gaps with a size below the used cutter radius are not critical.

Overlapping surfaces

Overlapping surfaces can result in unwanted rest material areas. These areas cannot be removed by Automatic rest material recognition, as they are not the result of too large cutter diameters but of intersecting surfaces.

In the course of, and after creating machining cycles, the CAD model must neither be moved nor changed in its position in space.

Negative allowances

The following restriction applies to all cycles: If the sum of the allowances is negative, the sum total must be less than the Tool radius - Machining tolerance.

Example:

Tool radius = 5.00 mm

Machining tolerance = 0.01 mm

Allowance = -3.0mm

Additional allowance XY must be greater than -1.99 mm, e.g. 1.98, because

|-3.00 mm +(-1.98 mm)| < 5.00 mm -0.01 mm

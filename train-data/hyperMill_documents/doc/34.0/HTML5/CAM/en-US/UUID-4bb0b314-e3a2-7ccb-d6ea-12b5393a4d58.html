3.11. Simulate machining

Machining can be simulated as follows.

  1. Using the internal simulation. See the information in section Internal simulation.

  2. With the Simulation Center.

  3. Using the hyperVIEW program. See the information in section Simulation in hyperVIEW.

  4. With the VIRTUAL Machining Center. See the information in the VIRTUAL Machining Center documentation.

  5. Using an external simulation program (for example, VERICUT). See the information in section External simulation.

Caution

OPEN MIND recommends the use of an NC code-based simulation such as the VIRTUAL Machining Center or an external simulation program to ensure the greatest possible degree of safety and collision protection.

To run an NC code-based simulation, an appropriate VIRTUAL Machining Center license is required. Please contact your OPEN MIND partner.

The internal simulation, the SIMULATION Center, and the simulation with hyperVIEW are not based on an NC file.

Machining safety

To machine safely with hyperMILL, please observe the following instructions.

Warning

Traversing movements on the clearance plane are not collision-checked.

This applies to all machining processes and cycle calculations as well as to simulation in simulations that are not NC code-based. Therefore the clearance plane must be placed at a sufficient distance above the surface of the component.

Machining planner

Start the VIRTUAL Machining Center in Machining planner mode and equip a turret for turning jobs with graphical support.

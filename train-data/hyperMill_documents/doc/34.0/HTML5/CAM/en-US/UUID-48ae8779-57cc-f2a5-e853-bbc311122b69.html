General information

The Milling area or Turning area defines the area of the CAD model to be machined that is checked for collisions at the same time. The Fixture area is also checked for collisions; this includes those areas of the CAD model that are used to fix the part during machining.

hyperMILL automatically generates a polyhedron model (3DF format) at a suitably high surface resolution (accuracy) for the Milling area, Turning area or Clamping area. For this purpose, the CAD model is subdivided into separate machining areas as required by the collision-checking function.

All subareas of the model not inside the milling area, turning area or clamping area are not collision-checked. Inside the milling area/turning area, you can exclude areas from machining (with the help of stop surfaces, for example).

The machining area within the milling area is defined

  • by defining a boundary (possible for all 3D and 5X cycles) or

  • by defining milling surfaces (possible for the following cycles: 3D / 5X Profile Finishing, 3D / 5X Z Level Finishing, 3D Z Level Shape Finishing and 3D / 5X Rest Machining).

Machining safety

To machine safely with hyperMILL, please observe the following instructions.

Warning

Traversing movements on the clearance plane are not collision-checked.

This applies to all machining processes and cycle calculations as well as to simulation in simulations that are not NC code-based. Therefore the clearance plane must be placed at a sufficient distance above the surface of the component.

OPEN MIND recommends the use of an NC code-based simulation such as the VIRTUAL Machining Center or an external simulation program to ensure the greatest possible degree of safety and collision protection.

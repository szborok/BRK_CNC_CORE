High Performance

A separate license is required for High Performance Rouging. Please contact your OPEN MIND partner.

With its High Performance Roughing, OPEN MIND provides a technology that is characterized by the following features:

  • Constant load on the tool and machine during the entire machining.

  • Feedrates that are adjusted dynamically to the machining situation

  • Machining is always performed using climb milling.

  • Feedrate for fast positioning movements

High Performance Roughing

High Performance Mode: Enable in order to use the High Performance Mode

Opening cut: Enable if an opening cut should be made.

Zigzag: Enable if machining with alternating orientation/cutting mode is required.

Two different strategies are available for High Performance Mode: Opening cut and Side mill only. The toolpaths created with hyperMILL are optimized for both strategies in terms of the minimum machining time at maximum removal rate, taking into account dynamic feedrate values for different machining situations.

The Fillet radius parameter must be at least 5% of the tool diameter.

Opening cut (1): If this option is enabled, narrow, inaccessible areas (A) with several vertical stepdowns (full cut) can be opened. A trochoidal strategy may be used. hyperMILL determines the most suitable strategy for the particular machining situation internally in terms of the shortest overall machining time.

Side mill only (2): If this option is enabled, no full cut behavior is allowed. Narrow, inaccessible areas (A) are opened with trochoidal movements.

Reposition

The following applies to the two strategies Opening cut and Side mill only:

Safety (1): axial movement (in the Z direction) away from the plane currently being machined. Default value: Clearance distance * 0.1.

Feedrate: Value for the maximum achievable feedrate for all movements to reposition the tool without material removal.

Infeed

Dense area stepover (factor) (1): Lateral infeed for trochoidal movements in narrow, inaccessible areas. The value is multiplied by the Stepover (factor of diameter). A reduction in the horizontal stepover in narrow areas may be particularly useful for hard materials.

Plunge feedrate: Minimum feedrate for plunge movement in closed pockets that cannot be accessed from the outside.

Opening cut

Adapt the vertical stepdown and the feedrate to the particular machining situation.

Vertical stepdown (1): The number of stepovers for the vertical stepdown is calculated from the vertical stepdown defined on the Parameters tab (total infeed).

Example: If the total infeed is 2 and the vertical stepdown for the opening cut is 0.5, machining occurs in 4 infeeds.

Feedrate: Maximum feedrate for the opening cut.

Min. feedrate: Enable to specify the minimum feedrate for the opening cut.

Note

A change in the feedrate for the opening cut may change the machining strategy for narrow areas (trochoidal > full cut). hyperMILL determines the most suitable strategy internally in terms of the shortest overall machining time, taking into account the feedrate value.

Entry feedrate

A targeted feedrate adjustment at the moment of entry into the material can be especially useful with hard workpiece materials.

Factor side entry regulates the lateral approach (not when the position is tangential to the contour) from outside and is referenced to the feedrate XY of the tool.

Factor plunge entry affects the helical or ramp plunge into the material from above. This factor is calculated with plunge feedrate.

Zigzag

Zag feedrate (factor): Factor influencing the feedrate of the opposite direction. If climb milling is chosen as cutting mode, the opposite direction feedrate, i.e. the conventional milling feedrate, is reduced by the specified factor. The 0.8 factor means a feedrate reduction of 20%.

Limitations

If machining is performed in High Performance Mode, a number of standard functions in the Arbitrary Stock Roughing cycle are not available or are limited to a certain setting. In detail, the following default settings currently apply:

Strategy tab

Machining sequence

By default, stock removal of cavities or pockets takes place level-by-level. High Performance Roughing has therefore limited suitability for subsequent machining tasks as no pocket sorting takes place.

Planar mode

By default, infeed is performed optimized in.

Cutting mode

Machining is always performed using climb milling.

Toolpath fillets

For abrupt changes of direction, all toolpaths are rounded. The Radius for horizontal infeed parameter is determined automatically.

Full cut behavior

The Reduce feedrate during full cut option is not available. Instead, the feedrates are adjusted dynamically to the machining conditions. For the opening cut, a separate feedrate can also be defined (see High Performance tab).

Parameters tab

The Minimal stock removal and Finish pass all levels parameters are not supported.

Macros tab

A complete helical or ramp movement always occurs for the plunge macro, where the angle is reduced dynamically to create a complete ramp or helical path. The Helix radius parameter is not available. This value is determined automatically according to the particular machining situation.

Setup tab

NC parameters

The Min. pocket size option is not available. Pockets that are smaller than the tool diameter + 2* fillet radius are not taken into account during machining. The G2/G3 output is not available. By default, all movements are output as G1 commands.

The Use min. dist. G0 option is not available.

Check tool

For unresolvable collision: collision avoidance is not supported.

Other features

Parametric feedrate output is not supported in the NC file.

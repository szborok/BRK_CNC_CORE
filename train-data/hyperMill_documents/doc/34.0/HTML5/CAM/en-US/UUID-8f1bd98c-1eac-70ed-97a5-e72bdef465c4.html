Macros

Shroud surface macro

Additional entry distance: Tangential extension of the entry movement.

Link extension: The link extension defines the distance to the shroud surface where the infeed to the adjacent toolpath (side infeed) occurs. In most cases, extremely small values suffice.

Hub macro

Start distance, Edge clearance: To exclude the hub fillets and the hub surface from machining, you can raise the paths in the fillet area to a defined clearance area (1).

The paths are raised continually from a given start distance (2).

By reducing the feedrate, you can avoid potential tool overload caused by increased amounts of rest material in the transition area.

Lift off paths

The empty lift off paths parallel to the cutting toolpath run at the level of the edge clearance. You can adjust the feedrate for these paths to the empty cutting condition.

Geometry

Basic geometry

Blade surfaces (1): Blade surfaces between shroud and hub. Define the tool orientation and must be part of the 3DF model. They normally correspond to the blade surfaces to be milled.

The blade surfaces must border each other cleanly and may not have any gaps. If the blade surfaces to be milled do not fulfil these conditions or they result in tool movements that are not necessary for milling, then alternative surfaces (in the sense of guide surfaces) can be selected that optimally fulfil these conditions.

Hub surface/ Shroud surface (2): On the blade surfaces of adjacent platform surfaces at the bottom/shroud of the blade.

Hub curve/ Shroud curve (3): The (closed!) profiles for the platform surfaces limit the machining area along the platform. For example, the following can be used as limits:

  • the surface boundary of the blade

  • any positioned/oriented types of cut through the blade surfaces

  • the surface boundary of the platform

Leading edge curves (pair) (4)/ Trailing edge curves (pair) (5): Curves that separate the edge area from the suction and pressure side. They can be calculated automatically by hyperMILL.

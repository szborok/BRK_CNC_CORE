Macros

Plunge macro

Helical: Use to remove the stock of 3D pockets.

Helix radius (1): Offset of the cutter center point relative to the axis of the helix (Z axis through plunge point).

Angle (2): Lead angle of the helix.

The number of tool revolutions during the infeed movement is the result of the value of the vertical stepdown and the lead angle of the helix. The direction of the helix is defined by the selected cutting mode. In tight spaces, collisions with neighboring surfaces are possible in theory. The macro is still executed close to the surface. It is more advantageous to select ramp-shaped plunging in such cases.

Ramp: Ramping-in takes place along the first milling path.

Angle (3): Enter the lead angle of the ramp.

None (4) The infeed is executed as a linear movement in the Z direction of the tool axis. To be used for:

  • Soft materials (aluminum, plastics),

  • Vertical insertion movements with a tool appropriate for vertical chip removal.

Plunge points: Start or plunge points of machining by specifying plunge points. Please note that the defined plunge points cannot cannot be considered in every situation for performance reasons.

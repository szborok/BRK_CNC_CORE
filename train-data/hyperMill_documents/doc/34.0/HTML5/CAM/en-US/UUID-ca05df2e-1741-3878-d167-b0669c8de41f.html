Macros

Approach macro/Retract macro

Radius: The rounding radius for tool approach and retract movements.

Additional axial distance: Increases the approach and retract path of the macros in the tool axis direction.

Axial feedrate

Feedrate: You can define the feedrate for the macro movement in the axial direction separately. It is usually preferable to use a higher feedrate here.

Link macro

In the case of the zigzag strategy, approach and retract macros are connected by a collision-controlled, simultaneous movement of all axes.

Radius, height: The radius defines the smooth transition into the lateral movement. The height defines the axial retraction of the milling tool during the transition movement to the next path.

Note

To protect the tool tip, the height should be greater than the scallop height that has been generated.

Smooth connect factor

A smooth transition with horizontal tool steps creates a greater curve radius in the area of the leading and trailing edge. This reduces both machining times and machine wear.

Leading edge / Trailing edge: The smooth transition can be controlled separately for the Leading and Trailing edge and can assume values between 0 and 1. Values between 0.2 and 0.3 are useful. The greater the value, the greater the transition radius. 0 disables the function and produces the behavior known so far.

Vertical steps: The vertical transitions can also be linked smoothly. In this, the cycle calculates the best possible link automatically on the basis of the component geometry and the defined settings.

Macros

Define the border behavior of the tool during machining. The following macros are available: approach macro, retract macro and plunge macro.

Retract macro

None: The tool approach/retraction takes place in the Z direction (1).

When defining an approach or retract macro, the approach takes place at the starting point and the retraction from the endpoint in the workplane:

Circular: in a quarter circle. Enter arc radius (2).

Perpendicular: perpendicular to contour tangent. Enter length (3).

Plunge macro

Tool movement for stepdown to the next workplane.

Not available in Stock mode.

None (A): the infeed movement is executed as a linear movement in Z direction. To be used for:

Use the vertical plunge macro for:

  • Stepdown without material removal (user-defined starting point is outside the stock)

  • Soft materials (aluminum, plastics),

  • Existing predrilled hole at starting point,

  • Vertical plunging with a tool suitable for vertical chip removal

Helical (B): Use to clear pockets without pre-drilling.

Helix radius (1): Offset of the cutter center point relative to the axis of the helix (Z axis through infeed point).

Angle (2): lead angle of the helix. Lead angle of the helix. The number of revolutions executed by the tool in the course of the infeed movement is computed from the value of the vertical stepdown and the lead angle of the helix.

Ramp (C): Ramping-in proceeds along the first milling path. Use the ramp plunge macro for soft free cutting in tight spaces.

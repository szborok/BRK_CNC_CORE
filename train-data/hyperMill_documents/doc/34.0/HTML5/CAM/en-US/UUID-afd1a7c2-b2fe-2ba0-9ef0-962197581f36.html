9. 2D Machining

9.1. Available machining cycles

Pocket Milling: Machining perpendicular pocket walls and adaptive pockets with automatic island recognition and options to calculate rest material areas.

Contour Milling: Milling open and closed 2D contours with optional path compensation and different approach and retract strategies. Rest material areas can be calculated.

Contour Milling on 3D Model: Milling open and closed contours with collision check, optional stop surfaces and automatic approach and retract strategies.

T-Slot Milling on 3D Model: Roughing and finishing of T-Slots along plane contours. Different axial infeed modes allow a free definition of Z infeed. Multiple tool reference points ensure precise milling results. Collision control and infeed movements based on a 3D model.

Chamfer Milling on 3D Model: Machining of prismatic components. It is possible to differentiate between the Modelled chamfer and Deburr / chamfer sharp edges machining strategies.

Inclined Contouring: Machining same as for Contour Milling, but with inclined pocket wall.

Inclined Pocketing: Machining same as for Pocket Milling, but with inclined pocket wall.

Rectangular Pocket: Machining rectangular pockets parallel to the contour with specifiable machining direction using climb milling or conventional milling.

Rest Machining: Machining rest material areas left after Pocket or Contour Milling.

Face Milling: Roughing of larger surfaces with parallel cuts within one or more contours, optionally with several steps in Z direction.

Playback Milling: Milling on toolpaths, manually generated with mouse interaction in a plane.

Plunge Milling: Guided by a contour, the cycle generates a Plunge Milling toolpath. Option to perform collision check and stock model update.

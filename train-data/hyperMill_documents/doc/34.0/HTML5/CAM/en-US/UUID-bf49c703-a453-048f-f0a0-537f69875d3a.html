Macros

Plunge macro

Various plunge strategies for infeed movement to the next machining level are available for all level-by-level machining cycles (except Z Level Finishing).

Axial (A): The infeed is executed as a linear movement in Z direction.

Use the axial plunge macro for:

  • Infeed without stock removal (user-defined plunge point is outside of stock or boundary).

  • Pre-drilling at the existing user-defined plunge point.

  • Axial plunging with a tool suitable for vertical chip removal.

Helical (B): used to remove the stock of 3D pockets. Helix radius (1): offset of the cutter center point relative to the axis of the helix (= Z axis through plunge point). Angle (2): lead angle of the helix. The number of revolutions executed by the tool in the course of the infeed movement is computed from the value of the vertical stepdown and the lead angle of the helix. The helix direction is defined by the selected orientation (climb/conventional milling).Lead angle of the helix.

3D stereo display

Set 3D stereo display.

View → 3D stereo display

Enable and configure 3D stereo display for VR goggles (active) and 3D glasses (passive).

Mode: Select a display mode depending on the graphics card and glasses. Note any information from the hardware manufacturer.

Table 75. Supported modes

Mode

Active / passive

Notes

Stereoscopic effect of graphics card (active)

Active glasses

Configure the stereoscopic effect of the graphics card to suit your monitor, glasses and computer. OpenGL quad-buffered stereo is common. The half screens for the left and right eye are typically shown alternately at the same position.

Horizontal half screen

Passive glasses with polarized glass

Two half screens are above or next to one another. Calculation undertaken by the software.

Vertical half screen

Anaglyph red cyan

Passive glasses with colored glass

Two half screens are colored in two complementary colors and laid above one another. Calculation undertaken by the software.

Anaglyph green magenta

Anaglyph blue yellow



Activate: Activate stereoscopic display.

Swap eyes: Swap the left and right image if the stereoscopic effect is not displayed correctly when using hardware OpenGL quad-buffered stereo.

Camera offset: Use the camera offset to configure the model depth, i.e., the separation between the left and right camera. The camera offset represents the separation between the eyes of an observer. Change the offset if the stereoscopic effect is not displayed correctly.

Zero parallax balance: The parallax makes it possible to correct the scene distortion in relation to the monitor level. Adjust the parallax at the monitor level so that a point of an object appears at exactly the same position in the images for both the left and the right eye.

Note

The perspective projection in Toggle projection is recommended to improve the realism of the scene. It is recommended to set the synchronization of the monitor to a refresh rate greater than 120 Hz.

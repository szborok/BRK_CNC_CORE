2.1. Holder/extension positional orientation

The alignment around the Z axis will allow non-symmetrical entities, such as drill heads and angle heads, to be supported in the future. If a holder (for example, HSK, CAPTO, KM and KM4X) is also used for turning on a mill/turn machine, the alignment around the Z axis must be observed. In the case of usual milling applications with circumferentially rotated bodies, the alignment around the Z axis is only a visual/aesthetic issue.

BT

The X axis points towards the center of one of the two slots . As the BT is symmetrical, the alignment of the X axis can only be distinguished with other attributes (for example, a screw or another non-symmetrical entity). Check the alignment of this entity before the 3D data import on the machine tool. The upper coupling should be defined in the flange depth.

SK

The X axis points towards the center of the flattest slot – on the same side as the notch . The upper coupling should be defined in the flange depth (3.2 mm above the upper side of the flange).

CAT

The X axis points towards the center of the flattest slot . The upper coupling should be defined in the flange depth (3.2 mm above the upper side of the flange).

HSK

The X axis points towards the center of the flattest slot – on the same side as the notch . The upper coupling should be defined on the upper side of the flange.

CAPTO

The Y axis points towards the center of the reference notch . The upper coupling should be defined on the upper side of the flange.

KM

The Y axis is aligned through the axis of the holes and points towards the recess on the tip (see arrow). The upper coupling should be defined on the upper side of the flange.

KM4X

The X axis is aligned through the center of the two lower slots and points in the same direction as the notch . The upper coupling should be defined on the upper side of the flange.
